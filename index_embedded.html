Request a New Tool

ALL NEW ANALYTICS TOOL PROCUREMENT REQUESTS NEED TO GO THROUGH THIS FAST-TRACK PROCESS.

This single entry point ensures you'll get expert guidance, Brambles will avoid tool duplication and tool sprawl, and our data security posture will improve over time. Feel free to drop into our analytics tool Teams channel to raise questions or explore requirements.

Analytics tool team channel ...
1

Submit your request and describe your needs

Complete the short request form describing what you need. It doesn't have to be a tool request per se, it could be a request relating to an analytics capability you feel you're missing. The analytics tool team will get in touch within 48 hours to arrange a discovery call. We'd like to understand your use case, timelines, and any specific requirements so we can find the best solution for you.

2

We match you with existing tools - or get approval to acquire the new capability

The analytics tooling team recently built an inventory of data and analytics tools in use across Brambles, for all specialisms including visualisation, BI development, data engineering, data science, machine learning and agentic AI. Frequently we'll be able to connect you with an existing solution that meets your needs immediately, often tools you didn't know we already had. Where no suitable tool exists, we'll seek approval to fill the gap, and initiate the evaluation and procurement where appropriate.

3

We handle the business case, compliance and enterprise-wide aspects, so you don't have to

For new tools, we'll help with Brambles-wide aspects such as investment appraisal, collecting evidence of demand for the new capability across our data and analytics communities, and navigating technology, security and privacy compliance requirements. We'll engage with potential vendors, evaluate products against requirements and arrive at a recommendation.

4

Enterprise-wide tool onboarding and set up

If you have a need for a new analytics tool, it's highly likely that other Brambles colleagues have the same need. Once a new tool is approved, we need to bring it into Brambles in a way that ensures it is available across the company, and is integrated with the correct systems and processes. There will also be training and learning requirements for new users, and these also need to be coordinated on a Brambles-wide basis.

In-scope Users

Primary Focus segments represent the most intense tool user bases, in terms of complexity or frequency of use. It is the tools used by these groups that will receive the greatest focus from the governance work, as we identify and implement a path forward to a converged tooling environment for analytics.

Secondary Focus groups represent a lower intensity of analytics tool use but because numerically they are larger than the primary groups, it may be that they drive a significant portion of our tool budget spend. So we will scan the secondary focus groups, albeit at a more superficial level, to discover more about which tools they use and to identify potential synergies with the needs of the primary focus groups.

Analytics Producers
Data Scientists & ML Engineers c. 70 people Primary Focus

Practitioners who develop advanced predictive models, machine learning algorithms, and statistical analyses. They apply experimental scientific methods to extract deep insights from complex datasets, creating solutions that power automated decision-making and artificial intelligence capabilities across Brambles.

Analytics Producers c. 110 people Primary Focus

Visual storytellers who craft dashboards, reports, and interactive visualizations. They transform complex data into intuitive visual insights using BI tools, creating consumption-ready analytics assets that communicate trends, metrics, and KPIs to drive business understanding and decision-making.

Analytics Process Specialists c. 120 people Primary Focus

Bridge-builders between technical and business domains who optimize analytics workflows. They translate business requirements into technical specifications, ensure data quality, implement automation, facilitate analytics adoption, and create processes that make analytics accessible to non-technical users.

Analytics Platform Builders c. 50 people Primary Focus

Technical architects who design and maintain the foundational data infrastructure. They develop robust data pipelines, implement governance frameworks, and ensure reliable data processing through ETL processes, data warehouses, and cloud platforms that enable analytics at scale.

Analytics Consumers
Power Analytics Consumers c. 550 people Primary Focus

Mid to senior-level leaders who actively use analytics to guide departmental strategies. They leverage purpose-built analytics solutions to identify opportunities, optimize resource allocation, measure performance, and drive continuous improvement within their functional areas of responsibility.

Operational Analytics Consumers c. 3,920 people Secondary Focus

Mid-level professionals who rely on standardized analytics for day-to-day operational decisions. They regularly monitor performance metrics, identify short-term trends, and use operational dashboards to manage resources, workflows, and business processes efficiently within established parameters.

Analytics-enabled Leaders c. 400 people Out of Scope

Executives and senior leaders who leverage sophisticated analytics to drive enterprise strategy. They consume executive dashboards, scenario models, and predictive analytics to make high-impact decisions that shape organizational direction, investment priorities, and transformational initiatives.

Minimal Analytics Users c. 9,300 people Out of Scope

Frontline staff who interact with simplified analytics interfaces. They access pre-defined metrics relevant to their specific roles through basic dashboards, mobile apps, and structured reports, primarily consuming rather than producing analytics content with minimal customization needs.
